¶879. Profitable Schemes

Difficulty: Hard

There are G people in a gang, and a list of various crimes they could commit.

The i-th crime generates a profit[i] and requires group[i] gang members to participate.

If a gang member participates in one crime, that member can’t participate in another crime.

Let’s call a profitable scheme any subset of these crimes that generates at least P profit, and the total number of gang members participating in that subset of crimes is at most G.

How many schemes can be chosen?  Since the answer may be very large, return it modulo 10^9 + 7.

Note:

  1. 1 <= G <= 100
  2. 0 <= P <= 100
  3. 1 <= group[i] <= 100
  4. 0 <= profit[i] <= 100
  5. 1 <= group.length = profit.length <= 100

¶Solution

dp[g][p]代表人数为g时，获利至少为p的总方案数。

官方题解:

cur[p][g], the number of schemes with profitability pp and requiring gg gang members: except we'll say (without changing the answer) that all schemes that profit at least P dollars will instead profit exactly P dollars.
当p=P时，代表的不是确定的利润了，而是代表所有大于等于P的利润。另外当人数大于G时是无效方案，所以对于人数数组只须开到G+1。
